# Modules

This document summarizes the main functional areas currently represented in PREATOR.

## Home / Cockpit

The home area acts as an operational summary layer. Its purpose is to surface the most relevant signals, priorities and quick actions without forcing the user to navigate blindly across the product.

## Inventory

The inventory area is centered on stock visibility and structure. This includes products, stock context, warehouse-related organization and movement traceability.

## Purchases

The purchasing area covers workflows such as purchase orders, receipts, supplier-side document flows and related operational follow-up.

## Sales & Finance

This area includes commercial and financial document flows such as quotes, delivery notes and invoices, together with financial visibility and operational coherence around these processes.

## Agenda

The agenda module supports planning, reminders and time-based operational visibility, with the intent of connecting tasks, due items and business timing.

## KPIs / Analytics

The KPI area is designed to surface business metrics and structured visibility rather than raw data alone.

## Integrations

The integrations area provides the basis for external connectivity, including webhook-oriented patterns and operational monitoring around them.

## Billing & Settings

This area groups subscription logic, plan visibility, billing UX, team configuration and organization-level settings.

## General product approach

The product is not built as a collection of isolated admin pages. A major design goal is cross-module coherence: navigation, naming, states and workflows should feel part of one system.
